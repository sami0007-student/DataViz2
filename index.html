US Presidential Election: Learning from 2016 & 2020

The United States 2020 Presidential Election has unseated the incumbent Donald J. Trump. What has Trump done in the last 4 years? 1, he managed to sign an executive order that bans people visiting the United States from Iran, Iraq, Somalia, Libya, Sudan, Syria and Yemen for 90 days. 2, he was trialed for impeachment on abuse of power. 3, he separated children from their parents at the US-Mexico Border, it resulted in more than the deportation of 500 children without their parents.

Joe Biden is the President-Elect that has managed to outdone Trump at the polls by a total of 5,975,789 votes. How did he manage to shorten the Trump administration into a one-term presidency? We will dive deep into the nitty-gritty of flipped states, voter turnout, and demographic trends to achieve a comprehensive understanding.

In 2016, the jokes about Donald Trump as President were gaining momentum, nobody expected Americans to vote him into office. Until, he won the election. Whilst Hillary Clinton won the popular vote by 65.84mil votes, a difference of 2.86mil votes to Trump. How? In states with many electoral votes, Trump won by a slim margin. Florida (with 29 electoral votes) sided with Trump by a margin of 1%, Pennsylvania (20 electoral votes) by a margin of 1.1%, and Michigan (16 electoral votes) by 0.3%.

In 2020, despite the severity of a global pandemic, the 2020 election was a highly-anticipated event. Evident in the highest voter turnout of 65.1% in over 100 years! More than half of the states saw increased participation, successfully voting Biden into office. Biden won key states such as Pennsylvania (20 electoral votes) with a margin of 2.8%, Michigan (16 electoral votes) with a margin of 5.1%, Georgia (16 electoral votes) with a margin of 1.3%. These were the states that voted in favour of Trump in 2016.

Flipped States - Democrat Takeover (2016 to 2020)

Swing states are when neither political party has ever been the consistent choice, voters in swing states hold a higher probability of deciding the presidential election. In contrast to safe states, which are states with expected leads in a political party. Arizona (11 electoral votes) had voted Republican in 10 of the past 11 elections. Georgia (16 electoral votes) had voted Republican in 8 out of the past 11 elections. A surprising flipped of events. Arizona, the home state of conservative Barry Goldwater, had not voted for Democrat since 1996. In 2020, voter turnout increased by 10 percentage points since 2016, with more than 300,000 Democratic voters turned out. Similarly, Georgia had not voted Democrat since 1992. Yet, Biden won by 14,000 votes.

Whilst Clinton lost Michigan (16), Pennsylvania (20), and Wisconsin (10) in a close-call. These states have historically lean towards the Democratic nominee.

Voter Turnout: 2016 vs. 2020

Amidst social distancing safety, mail-in ballots and early in-person voting were favored to avoid the crowd. In 2020, 99mil people participated in early-voting, that's nearly three quarters of the entire 2016 voter turnout! The swing states saw an increase in voter turnout since 2016, states such as Minnesota nearly achieved 80% turnout of voters. The voter turnout in 2020 surpassed 2016, marginalized communities were especially galvanized this election to exercise their democratic rights.
